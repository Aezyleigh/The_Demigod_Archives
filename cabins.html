The Cabins of Camp Half-Blood

Discover cabins representing each Olympian god. Who’s your godly parent?

Cabin I — Zeus

God/Goddess: Zeus (Roman Name: Jupiter)
Title: King of the Gods, God of the Sky
Symbol:Master Bolt, Eagle
Domain & Power: Commands the sky, weather, and law. His children are natural leaders who can summon storms and wield lightning.
Cool Trivia: His sacred animal is the eagle, and he has a notorious soft spot for mortal women, which is why he has so many demigod children.

Cabin II — Hera

God/Goddess: Hera (Roman Name: Juno)
Title: Queen of the Gods, Goddess of Marriage
Symbol: Peacock, Lotus Staff
Domain & Power: Protector of marriage, women, and family. She does not have mortal children, so her cabin is for her champions.
Cool Trivia: She curses those who wrong her with brutal creativity. Percy Jackson was one of the few to ever be granted her blessing.

Cabin III — Poseidon

God/Goddess: Poseidon (Roman Name: Neptune) Title: God of the Seas, Earthshaker
Symbol: Trident, Horse
Domain & Power: Rules the oceans, storms, and earthquakes. His children can breathe underwater, communicate with sea life, and control waves.
Cool Trivia: He created horses out of sea foam as a gift for humanity and has a famous rivalry with Athena.

Cabin IV — Demeter

God/Goddess: Demeter (Roman Name: Ceres)
Title: Goddess of Agriculture
Symbol: Golden Sheaf of Wheat
Domain & Power: Commands all plants, harvests, and the cycle of life. Her children are expert gardeners with chlorokinesis (plant control).
Cool Trivia: Her depression over her daughter Persephone's absence causes winter each year.

Cabin V — Ares

God/Goddess: Ares (Roman Name: Mars)
Title: God of War
Symbol: Boar, Spear, Bloodstained Helm
Domain & Power: Embodies the brutal, chaotic aspects of war. His children are fierce warriors who excel in combat and can curse weapons.
Cool Trivia: He drives a massive, gun-metal gray motorcycle and has a secret, soft spot for Aphrodite.

Cabin VI — Athena

God/Goddess: Athena (Roman Name: Minerva)
Title: Goddess of Wisdom and Strategic Warfare
Symbol: Owl, Olive Tree
Domain & Power: Born from Zeus's head, she is the patron of wisdom, craft, and strategy. Her children are brilliant inventors and master strategists.
Cool Trivia: Her children are born literally as ideas, springing fully formed from their mother's mind.

Cabin VII — Apollo

God/Goddess: Apollo (Roman Name: Apollo)
Title: God of the Sun, Archery, and Prophecy
Symbol: Lyre, Sun Chariot, Bow and Arrow
Domain & Power: Lord of the sun, truth, music, and healing. His children are expert archers, healers, and sometimes receive the gift of prophecy.
Cool Trivia: He is a fan of haikus and once became a mortal named Lester Papadopoulos as punishment.

Cabin VIII — Artemis

God/Goddess: Artemis (Roman Name: Diana)
Title: Goddess of the Hunt and the Moon
Symbol: Bow and Arrow, Silver Moon
Domain & Power: A maiden goddess who reigns over the hunt, wild animals, and the moon. She leads the Hunters of Artemis, a group of immortal maidens.
Cool Trivia: She granted her lieutenant, Zoë Nightshade, immortality and turned her into a constellation.

Cabin IX — Hephaestus

God/Goddess: Hephaestus (Roman Name: Vulcan)
Title: God of the Forge and Fire
Symbol: Hammer and Anvil
Domain & Power: Master blacksmith and craftsman of the gods. His children are incredible inventors and builders, creating automatons and magical items.
Cool Trivia: He built the bronze giant Talos and the intricate traps of the Labyrinth.

Cabin X — Aphrodite

God/Goddess: Aphrodite (Roman Name: Venus)
Title: Goddess of Love and Beauty
Symbol: Mirror, Dove, Scarf
Domain & Power: Born from sea foam, she influences love, desire, and beauty. Her children can sense and manipulate emotions, often excuding charm and charisma.
Cool Trivia: Her true divine form is so beautiful it can blind mortals. She thinks Paris made the right choice.

Cabin XI — Hermes

God/Goddess: Hermes (Roman Name: Mercury)
Title: God of Travelers, Thieves, and Messengers
Symbol: Caduceus (Winged Staff)
Domain & Power: The messenger of the gods, he rules over roads, trade, and thieves. His children are naturally agile, persuasive, and skilled with locks.
Cool Trivia: His cabin is always the most crowded, as it houses all unclaimed and minor-god children.

Cabin XII — Dionysus

God/Goddess: Dionysus (Roman Name: Bacchus)
Title: God of Wine and Madness
Symbol: Thyrsus (Pine-Cone Staff), Grapevine
Domain & Power: Master of wine, celebration, and ritual madness. His children have powers of vitakinesis (healing) and can induce madness or manipulate plants.
Cool Trivia: He is the current director of Camp Half-Blood, a punishment from Zeus for chasing an off-limits nymph. He hates his job.
